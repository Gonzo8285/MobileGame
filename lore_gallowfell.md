# The Curse of Gallowfell — lore foundation v0.1

## Premise
Gallowfell was the Monarchy's national gallows-town. For centuries every condemned prisoner from the empire was marched here to hang. Eventually the cumulative weight of so many wrongful deaths — plus one execution that should never have happened — cursed the town. The dead refuse to leave. Reality frays. The town is now the fault-line of the empire.

## The town as biome map (chapter biomes)
- **Cathedral Ruins** — Iron Penitents home; a basilica that lost its god mid-sermon and forgot to stop.
- **The Ash Quarter** — Ash-Mourners home; the burned chapels and crematoria from the empire's last plague-year.
- **The Forgotten Parade** — The Last Legion home; the imperial drill-yard where they were ordered to hold and then no orders ever came.
- **The Black Mire** — Coven of the Black Mire home; the bog at the town's south wall, fed by execution-water for centuries.
- **The Cinderwood** — Skinward Pact home; the half-burnt forest north of the wall, regrowing wrong.
- **The Gallows Hill** — chapter-end boss biome; the scaffold-tree at the town's peak. Taller than the cathedral.

## Why each faction marches into Gallowfell
- **Iron Penitents** — pilgrimage. The Monarchy's national gallows is the holiest site of suffering they know. They walk in to flagellate themselves bloody on the same stones the condemned bled on. The curse looks like a bargaining tool.
- **Ash-Mourners** — the dead of Gallowfell were never properly mourned. The Mourners came to do the rites the empire was too busy to do. The curse, they argue, is grief that never had a funeral. They mean to bury it.
- **Coven of the Black Mire** — the curse is *eating* the spirit-tide their bog used to drink. Their gods have gone quiet for the first time in a thousand years. They've come to find out where the souls are going, and to take some back if they can.
- **The Last Legion** — their final sealed orders, signed by an emperor long-dead, said: *"Hold Gallowfell against the dead."* They've never been told to stand down. They march in because they don't know how to march anywhere else.
- **Skinward Pact** — the gallows-tree was carved from a god-tree their ancestors worshipped. They came for the wood, the bones, and a generations-late vengeance. Some of their elders insist the tree is still listening. None of them want to find out what it has heard.

## Curse mechanics → game systems (lore-justified)
- **Reanimation** — defeated enemies in Gallowfell have a small % chance to rise once at 1 HP on their tile. Justifies the Ash-Mourners' resurrection identity AND escalates difficulty in late chapters.
- **The Hanging Hour** — every fight has an in-fiction "midnight" beat where all units (yours and enemies') gain +1 ATK. Drives mid-fight tension and reframes RNG as inevitability.
- **The Innocent Saint** — the one wrongful execution that triggered the curse. Lore-locked figure (TBD: name, faction). Reserved as hidden Warlord 11, unlocked when a player completes a campaign with all 10.
