## 3. The longer pitch (three paragraphs)

**The setting.** Gallowfell was the empire's national gallows-town. For centuries every condemned prisoner from the empire was marched there to hang, and centuries of cumulative wrongful deaths — plus one execution that never should have happened — finally cursed the place. The dead refuse to leave. Reality frays at the edges. Every fight has a moment, mid-combat, where the gallows-bell tolls the **Hanging Hour** and the world tilts toward the dead — units get angrier, fallen enemies sometimes rise on the spot, and the player has to decide whether that's an opportunity or a noose. Five factions march into the town for five different reasons: the **Iron Penitents** as pilgrims to suffering, the **Ash-Mourners** to drain the death-essence that keeps their court alive, the **Coven of the Black Mire** to follow their patron demon's hoofprints, **the Last Legion** because their sealed imperial orders said _hold Gallowfell against the dead_ and nobody has rescinded them, and the **Skinward Pact** to claim the felled god-tree the gallows was carved from.

**The play.** A run is eight sequential combats — no branching map, no chaff between fights. Each combat is sixty to ninety seconds. You draw five cards, pay mana that ramps from three to eight, and place units across three lanes against waves that escalate per-round. Between combats you take a reward card; at locked rest nodes you can upgrade or prune. Lose anywhere and the run is over; restart is free, retry costs gems. Across runs you accumulate Bones to spend on the **Ancestor Tree** — a sideways-power passive tree where every node is a tradeoff, never a flat strength gain — and per-Warlord **Ascension** levels A1 through A20, each rung adding a modifier that makes the eight-round march nastier. Eleven Warlords across five factions, three sub-archetypes each, fifteen viable deck-leans. The eleventh — the **Saint That Should Not Hang** — is lore-locked. You can never buy her.

**The business.** Free-to-play with one anti-pay-to-win invariant: nothing that affects gameplay is paid. All five free Warlords are free forever. Souls are an earned-only currency. The Ancestor Tree is Bones-only. The paid Warlords are reachable in Marrow Shards (run-end soft currency) or gems — gems buy time, never power. What's monetised is cosmetic: card treatments (Foil / Gold / Ink / Prism / Cursed / Ultimate), Warlord skins beyond the free mastery skin, card-backs, board skins, summon-VFX skins, alt-arts, and the 30-day Marrow Pass with its single hero reward — one season-exclusive Cursed-treatment of one card. PEGI 12 / Teen 13+ keeps the reach wide. No real-money loot boxes for power. Gacha pity at 80 pulls is disclosed up-front.

## 6. Features (8 bullets)

- **Eight-round runs, no filler.** Each run is eight sequential combats — three combat / one elite / one horde / one combat / one horde / one boss. Total run length: 8–15 minutes. Designed for one bus journey.
- **Eleven Warlords across five factions.** Five free, five paid (reachable in soft currency or gems), one lore-locked. Each anchors a playstyle: aggro, control, swarm, tempo, summoner, plus five hybrids.
- **The Hanging Hour.** A mid-combat narrative beat that fires on turn 5 (turn 4 vs boss). Bell tolls, screen ash-tints, all units gain +1 ATK, and the curse rolls Reanimation odds. You learn to play around it, not beat it.
- **Sideways-only meta-progression.** The Ancestor Tree's thirty-to-forty nodes are tradeoffs — _+1 starting Gold for −1 starting HP_, never strict power. Anti-pay-to-win bound, anti-power-creep bound.
- **Ascension A1–A20 per Warlord.** Each rung adds a modifier; milestone rungs (A5/A11/A16/A20) add Warlord-specific narrative beats and unlock a final mastery cosmetic at A20.
- **Cosmetic-only economy.** Seven card treatments × three alt-arts per card × per-Warlord skins × card-backs × board skins × summon-VFX skins. The cosmetic surface is the whale ceiling; the gameplay surface is fully free.
- **Five-faction folklore + grimdark register.** Iron Penitents, Ash-Mourners, Coven of the Black Mire, the Last Legion, Skinward Pact. Five biomes, five leitmotifs (D-minor Penitents' March / F#-minor Court That Won't Die / 7/8 Bog Remembers / C-minor Forge Discipline / 5/4 Cinderwood Howl). PEGI 12.
- **Marrow Pass + Ascendant Pact.** 30-day, 50-tier, dual-track season pass with a single hero reward: one season-exclusive Cursed-treatment of a card. Plus an optional subscription that adds an XP multiplier, daily gem trickle, and a monthly cosmetic — gated to post-D14 to avoid drowning the Pass.

## 7. What makes it different (three short paragraphs)

**It refuses to be a slot machine.** The mobile midcore market is full of games where the gacha is the loop. Gallowfell's gacha is the cosmetic wardrobe. The eleventh Warlord — the curse herself, the **Saint That Should Not Hang** — has _no IAP path_. You unlock her by beating the campaign with all ten others. We say "anti-pay-to-win invariant" in design documents the way other studios say "engagement layer." Every commit that touches a money path is audited against six guardrails defined up-front in `monetisation_map.md`.

**The setting commits.** Most mobile decks are a kit of mechanics with a paint job. Gallowfell is a place — a specific, named, mapped town that the curse rewrote into five districts, each one a faction biome. Music is being composed _per district_, with Wardruna-folk-doom in the Cinderwood, a Catholic-procession choral in the Catacombs, and a forge-discipline brass section under the Last Legion's Foundry, plus Mick-Gordon-energy industrial percussion under the chapter bosses. Story beats fire diegetically: the Hanging Hour is a literal bell-toll, not a buff icon.

**A studio of one human and an AI staff.** That's not a marketing line; it's the operating model. The studio openly bets on the modern small-team thesis: an experienced lead plus disciplined AI agents across every craft can deliver at a quality bar that used to require fifteen people. The risk-register lists "AI-art-pipeline disclosure" as a managed item, not a hidden one. You can ask us about it. We have answers.
